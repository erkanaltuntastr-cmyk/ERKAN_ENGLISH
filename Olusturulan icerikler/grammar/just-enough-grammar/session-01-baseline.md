# Session 01 - Baseline

## Learner Context

Erkan has studied grammar many times over about 30 years, but the knowledge has faded because it was not used and repeated enough.

Current situation:
- Lives in the UK.
- Speaks English in daily work.
- Works in the food service sector.
- Customers are mostly Turkish restaurants.
- Main need: clearer spoken English for work conversations, orders, customer relationships, and AI/technical interests.

Initial self-assessment:
- Vocabulary is probably not the main weakness.
- Main weak areas seem to be connectors, prepositions, fixed expressions, and tense control.
- Spoken English feels broken even though communication is happening.

## Original Writing Sample

I'm working in food service sector in th uk. My customers mostly turkish restaurants. My main job is speaking with them and create regular order system. Simply if a restaurant gives regular weekly to me it'sa good sign. i have salary and commisions on their monthly payments. also im interesting ai hipe and trying to learn as much as from ai.

## Corrected Version

I work in the food service sector in the UK. Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants. My main job is to speak with them and help them create a regular ordering system. Put simply, if a restaurant places a regular weekly order with me, that is a good sign. I earn a salary and commission based on their monthly payments. I am also interested in the AI hype, and I am trying to learn as much as I can from AI.

## More Natural Work Version

I work in the food service sector in the UK, mainly with Turkish restaurants. My job is to speak with restaurant owners and help them set up regular weekly orders. If a customer starts ordering every week, that is a good sign for me because I earn a salary plus commission based on their monthly payments. I am also very interested in AI and I am trying to learn as much as I can from it.

## Key Corrections

| Original | Better | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I'm working in food service sector | I work in the food service sector | Use present simple for regular work; add `the`. |
| in th uk | in the UK | Fixed spelling, article, and capitalisation. |
| My customers mostly Turkish restaurants | Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants | Missing verb `are`; more natural word order. |
| My main job is speaking with them and create | My main job is to speak with them and help them create | Keep parallel structure after `is`: `to speak` + `help`. |
| create regular order system | create a regular ordering system | Needs article; `ordering system` is the natural noun phrase. |
| gives regular weekly to me | places a regular weekly order with me | Correct collocation: `place an order`; correct preposition: `with me`. |
| i have salary and commisions | I earn a salary and commission | Work/payment collocation; `commission` is often singular/uncountable here. |
| on their monthly payments | based on their monthly payments | More precise expression. |
| im interesting ai hipe | I am interested in the AI hype | `interested in`, not `interesting`; fixed spelling. |
| as much as from ai | as much as I can from AI | Missing subject + modal phrase. |

## Early Diagnosis

Likely strengths:
- You can communicate the core message.
- You have enough vocabulary to explain your job and interests.
- You can form basic clauses without getting completely stuck.

Likely weak areas:
- Present simple vs present continuous.
- Articles: `a`, `an`, `the`, zero article.
- Prepositions: `in`, `with`, `based on`, `from`.
- Collocations: `place an order`, `earn commission`, `set up a system`.
- Sentence linking and punctuation.
- Parallel structures: `to speak and help`, not `speaking and create`.
- Capitalisation and spelling in written English.

## First Study Priorities

1. Present simple for routines and jobs.
2. Articles with work nouns and systems.
3. Prepositions for business relationships.
4. Work collocations around orders, customers, salary, and commission.
5. Short natural sentence patterns for speaking.

## Speaking Pattern To Practise

Use this pattern:

`I work in... My main job is to... Most of my customers are... If they..., it means...`

Example:

I work in the food service sector. My main job is to speak with restaurant owners and help them place regular weekly orders. Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants. If they order every week, it means the relationship is becoming stable.
