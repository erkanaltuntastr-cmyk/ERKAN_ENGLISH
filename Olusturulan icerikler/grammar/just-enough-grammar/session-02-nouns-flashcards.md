# Session 02 - Nouns Flashcards

Use these cards in three passes.

Pass 1: Look at the front, say the answer aloud, then check the back.

Pass 2: Cover the back and write the answer in a notebook or in a new practice file.

Pass 3: Use the answer in one original sentence about your real work.

## Set A - Core noun ideas

### Card 01

Front: What is a noun?

Back: A noun names a person, place, thing, group, idea, quality, action, or business concept.

Example: `customer`, `restaurant`, `order`, `quality`, `commission`, `relationship`.

### Card 02

Front: What is a noun phrase?

Back: A noun phrase is a noun plus the words that belong with it.

Example: `a regular weekly order`, `the restaurant owner`, `my regular customers`.

### Card 03

Front: Why are noun phrases important for my English?

Back: Because many natural work sentences depend on stable noun phrases, not advanced grammar.

Example: `I work in the food service sector` is stronger than `I work in food service sector`.

### Card 04

Front: What is a common noun?

Back: A common noun names a general person, place, thing, or idea.

Example: `customer`, `restaurant`, `order`, `invoice`, `delivery`.

### Card 05

Front: What is a proper noun?

Back: A proper noun names a specific person, place, company, brand, or organisation. It usually starts with a capital letter.

Example: `London`, `Erkan`, `Star Catering`, `the UK`.

### Card 06

Front: Which is correct: `I live in London` or `I live in the London`?

Back: `I live in London.`

Note: City names usually do not use `the`.

### Card 07

Front: Which is correct: `I live in UK` or `I live in the UK`?

Back: `I live in the UK.`

Note: Use `the` with `the UK` and `the United Kingdom`.

### Card 08

Front: What is a concrete noun?

Back: A concrete noun names something you can see, touch, taste, hear, or smell.

Example: `box`, `cheese`, `invoice`, `van`, `menu`, `warehouse`.

### Card 09

Front: What is an abstract noun?

Back: An abstract noun names an idea, quality, state, feeling, or business concept.

Example: `quality`, `trust`, `commission`, `support`, `communication`.

### Card 10

Front: What is a compound noun?

Back: A compound noun is made from two or more words that work together as one noun idea.

Example: `price list`, `delivery time`, `product quality`, `customer account`.

## Set B - Singular, plural, and countability

### Card 11

Front: What is a singular noun?

Back: A singular noun names one person, thing, place, idea, or unit.

Example: `a customer`, `an order`, `the invoice`.

### Card 12

Front: What is a plural noun?

Back: A plural noun names more than one person, thing, place, idea, or unit.

Example: `customers`, `orders`, `invoices`, `deliveries`.

### Card 13

Front: Correct this: `Most of my customer are Turkish restaurant.`

Back: `Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants.`

Note: `Most of my...` normally needs plural nouns when you are speaking about many people or businesses.

### Card 14

Front: What is the plural of `delivery`?

Back: `deliveries`.

Example: `We had two late deliveries this week.`

### Card 15

Front: What is the plural of `company`?

Back: `companies`.

Example: `Some companies pay monthly.`

### Card 16

Front: What is the plural of `box`?

Back: `boxes`.

Example: `The boxes arrived damaged.`

### Card 17

Front: What is a countable noun?

Back: A countable noun can be counted as separate units.

Example: `a customer`, `two customers`, `an order`, `several orders`.

### Card 18

Front: What is an uncountable noun?

Back: An uncountable noun is treated as a mass, material, general concept, or category, not as separate countable units.

Example: `food`, `information`, `advice`, `quality`, `commission`.

### Card 19

Front: Which is correct: `an information` or `some information`?

Back: `some information`.

Note: `Information` is uncountable.

### Card 20

Front: Which is correct: `a commission` or `commission` in this sentence: `I earn a salary and ____`?

Back: `I earn a salary and commission.`

Note: In this payment meaning, `commission` is often used as an uncountable category.

## Set C - Articles

### Card 21

Front: When do we use `a`?

Back: Use `a` before one general singular countable noun with a consonant sound.

Example: `a customer`, `a restaurant`, `a product`, `a system`.

### Card 22

Front: When do we use `an`?

Back: Use `an` before one general singular countable noun with a vowel sound.

Example: `an order`, `an invoice`, `an owner`, `an issue`.

### Card 23

Front: Correct this: `I want to create regular ordering system.`

Back: `I want to create a regular ordering system.`

Note: `System` is singular and countable, so it needs an article or another determiner.

### Card 24

Front: When do we use `the`?

Back: Use `the` when the noun is specific, already known, defined, or clear to both speaker and listener.

Example: `the customer`, `the order`, `the delivery`, `the UK`.

### Card 25

Front: Complete: `I work in ____ food service sector in ____ UK.`

Back: `I work in the food service sector in the UK.`

### Card 26

Front: First mention and second mention: `___ restaurant called me. ___ restaurant wants a new price list.`

Back: `A restaurant called me. The restaurant wants a new price list.`

### Card 27

Front: When do plural nouns use no article?

Back: Use no article when speaking generally about plural nouns.

Example: `Restaurants need reliable suppliers.`

### Card 28

Front: When do uncountable nouns use no article?

Back: Use no article when speaking generally about uncountable nouns.

Example: `Quality is important.`

### Card 29

Front: Correct this: `The customers want the clear prices` if the meaning is general.

Back: `Customers want clear prices.`

### Card 30

Front: Correct this: `A quality is important.`

Back: `Quality is important.`

Note: `Quality` is usually uncountable when used generally.

## Set D - Noun jobs in sentences

### Card 31

Front: What is the subject in this sentence? `The customer called me.`

Back: `The customer`.

Note: Ask `Who called me?`

### Card 32

Front: What is the direct object in this sentence? `The customer placed an order.`

Back: `an order`.

Note: Ask `Placed what?`

### Card 33

Front: What is the object of the preposition in this phrase? `with the customer`

Back: `the customer`.

Note: It comes after the preposition `with`.

### Card 34

Front: What is the subject complement in this sentence? `This product is a bestseller.`

Back: `a bestseller`.

Note: It comes after `is` and renames or describes the subject.

### Card 35

Front: Correct the possessive phrase: `the customer order`

Back: `the customer's order`.

### Card 36

Front: Correct the possessive phrase: `the restaurants orders` for many restaurants.

Back: `the restaurants' orders`.

## Set E - Work collocations with nouns

### Card 37

Front: Natural collocation: `give an order to me` or `place an order with me`?

Back: `place an order with me`.

### Card 38

Front: Complete: `A good customer places ____ regular weekly order ____ me.`

Back: `A good customer places a regular weekly order with me.`

### Card 39

Front: Complete: `My commission is based ____ monthly payments.`

Back: `My commission is based on monthly payments.`

### Card 40

Front: Better noun phrase: `price paper` or `price list`?

Back: `price list`.

### Card 41

Front: Better noun phrase: `customer relation` or `customer relationship`?

Back: `customer relationship`.

### Card 42

Front: Better noun phrase: `delivery problem` or `delivery issue`?

Back: Both can be correct. `Delivery issue` is often a little softer and more professional.

### Card 43

Front: Complete: `Product ____ is one of our strengths.`

Back: `Product quality is one of our strengths.`

### Card 44

Front: Complete: `The owner asked for the new ____ list.`

Back: `The owner asked for the new price list.`

### Card 45

Front: Correct this: `I have salary and commissions on monthly payments.`

Back: `I earn a salary and commission based on monthly payments.`

### Card 46

Front: Correct this: `My customers mostly Turkish restaurants.`

Back: `Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants.`

### Card 47

Front: Complete the pattern: `I work in... Most of my customers are... My job is to...`

Back: `I work in the food service sector. Most of my customers are Turkish restaurants. My job is to speak with restaurant owners and help them place regular weekly orders.`

### Card 48

Front: Make this noun phrase specific: `a customer`

Back: `the customer`, `my customer`, `this customer`, or `one of my regular customers`.

### Card 49

Front: Make this noun phrase plural and general: `the restaurant`

Back: `restaurants`.

Example: `Restaurants need reliable suppliers.`

### Card 50

Front: Make this noun phrase more natural: `regular order system`

Back: `a regular ordering system` or `a regular weekly ordering pattern`.
